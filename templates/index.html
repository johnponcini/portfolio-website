I was introduced to coding at a young age by my grandpa who had been an engineer at Hewlett Packard. To him, electronics and computing were more than just a hobby or career, they were a way of life. The seemingness endless cups of coffee and lines of C++ on one of the many desktop computers are testimony to this. And it wasn't long before I began coding myself. Since then, I've had the opportunity to build software tools for a synthetic biology competition, a pharmaceutical start-up, and a non-profit organization.

Recently, I've been fortunate enough to take some time away from my work to spend with my new family. These days I'm mostly taking care of my newborn twins. And I can honestly say that my two baby girls are everything to me.

Now, my interests have circled back to biology. I studied bioengineering at the University of California, Santa Cruz, and had dreams of starting my own biotech company. And that dream is still very much alive today.
